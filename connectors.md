# Connectors

Connectors let Orb use the things in your life — your calendar, reminders, and more — so it
can actually help instead of just chatting.

{: .note }
> Connector availability is expanding quickly. This page describes the model; check the app's
> Connectors screen for what's live in your version.

## How connectors work

- You turn a connector on and grant permission. Orb only uses what you've allowed.
- Your data stays on your device; it's shared with your chosen brain only to answer the thing
  you asked, in that moment.
- Connectors work whether you ask **by voice or by text**.

## On-device (your phone)

These use Apple's own permission prompts and keep the data on your phone:

- **Calendar** — "what's on my calendar tomorrow?", "am I free Friday afternoon?"
- **Reminders** — "what's on my list?", and (as write support rolls out) "remind me to…"
- **Contacts** — used to resolve who you mean when you ask about a person.

## Account connectors

Things like **Gmail** and **Google Calendar** connect through the provider's own secure
sign-in (you log in to Google, Orb never sees your password). These roll out over time.

## Reading vs. doing

- **Reading** (seeing your calendar, your reminders) is the common case and is safe by design.
- **Doing** things that change your data (creating an event or reminder) is always **confirm
  first** — Orb shows you exactly what it'll do and waits for your okay. It won't silently
  change things.

## Privacy

- You control every connector individually and can turn any of them off.
- Nothing is read until you ask something that needs it.
- A connector being "on" means *available to Orb when relevant* — not constantly watched.

## Want a connector that isn't here yet?

This is a community project — suggestions and contributions for new connectors are welcome.
Open an issue or PR on GitHub.
